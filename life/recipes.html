Jja Jang Myeon

  • use a wok
  • chun-jang
    • fry at low temperature
    • set aside oil to use in the remaining steps
  • jja-jang
    • fry oil and thinly sliced spring onions (a lot)
    • chopped pork into wok
    • whip soy sauce around the work making it burned
    • add chopped onion and cabbage
    • add sugar
    • add fried chun-jang
    • add water and boil
    • mix potato starch and water to make batter
    • slowly add the starch water until desired thickness
  • cook noodle
    • white and thick wheat
    • once cooked, quickly rince with cold water
  • pour jja-jang over noodle and serve

Korean Hot Dog

  • batter
    • strong white flour: 500g
    • water: 375ml
    • white sugar: 110g
    • baking powder: 12g
    • salt: 6g
    • msg: one tea spoon
  • skew
    • frankfurter (or similar) into thick wooden stick
    • thinly cut the skin (not to explode while frying)
  • frying
    • oil in deep and wide fan
    • heat oil to a low frying temp, e.g. 170 - 180C
    • first frying
      • cover skew with thin batter
      • fry until light brown. about 4 mins
    • send frying
      • cover skew with thin batter
      • roll over bread crumbs
      • fry until light brown. about 4 mints

Cotton Cheese Cake

  • based on this
  • prepare warm water
    • fill a tin with 1-2cm deep water. tin has to be wide enough to hold the cake tin.
    • put the tin into the oven
    • preheat sthe oven. 160C
  • 6 eggs: yellow and white separated
  • yellow batter
    • double-boil a bowl with
      • milk, 60g
      • butter, 50g
      • creamcheese, 160g
    • remove from heat
    • mix yellow egg parts into the batter
    • hand-mix until bubbles on top
    • cake flour, 50g
      • sieve into the batter
      • mix them. no need to use hand mixer
  • white batter
    • 10g white vinegar into white egg parts
    • mix using hand mixer
    • 75g sugar
    • add them very slowly/granually while hand mixing
    • until medium peak. don't make it to hard
  • In 3 steps, mix yellow and white
  • batter into a deep round tin
    • put baking paper at the bottom
    • pour the batter into the tin
    • smooth the top
  • baking
    • on bottom rack
    • cover with aluminium foil: no-fan 150C 1 hour
    • uncover: no-fan 150C 20 mins
    • oven at low broil: 2 mins
  • cooling: stand the tin on side
